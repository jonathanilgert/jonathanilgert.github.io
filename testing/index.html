Our process

Every batch, tested twice — once by the manufacturer, again by us.

Manufacturer testing is a starting point, not a standard. Wild Rose independently re-verifies every batch through two Canadian laboratories before a single vial is labelled. Here's exactly how it works.

Step 01

Confirm the batch at the source

We begin by obtaining written confirmation of the batch number and production records directly from the manufacturer, along with their third-party testing results. We review every figure they provide — but we don't stop there. Manufacturer testing isn't good enough for us, and it shouldn't be for you.

Step 02

Independent identity and purity testing (University of Alberta)

We send samples from the batch to the University of Alberta, where the material undergoes mass spectrometry to confirm identity and HPLC to measure purity. This verifies that the compound is exactly what it claims to be — and exactly how pure.

Step 03

Independent endotoxin and sterility testing (PBR)

From the same batch, we send a separate series of samples to PBR for endotoxin screening, confirming the product meets sterility thresholds. Same batch, two independent labs, two distinct layers of verification.

Step 04

Match every label to its results

Once testing is complete, we reprint every label with the correct batch number and match each batch directly to its laboratory results. The number on the vial ties to the testing for that specific lot — nothing generic, nothing recycled.

Step 05

Publish the Certificate of Analysis

Finally, we upload every Certificate of Analysis (COA) to our website. Look up any batch number and see the complete testing record for the exact product in your hand.

Batch-specific records

Certificate of Analysis (COA) hosting and QR verification

Each finished label points back to a batch-specific report record: lot number, product, laboratory result, report date, version, report file, and QR target. The lookup page keeps each report tied to the product in hand.
